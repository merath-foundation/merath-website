    MEMOIRS — THE LEDGER · WHAT HAPPENS, SET IN TIME

    merath works with what moves and changes: memory, objects, people, languages, and the relationships that take shape across place and time. A research and creative collective based in Tripoli, bringing together artists, researchers, writers, designers, and anthropologists, working between the Sahara and the Mediterranean within contexts shaped by intersecting social, cultural, and historical transformations.

    From books and exhibitions to research, films, and artworks, the forms of production at merath are shaped by the needs of each project.

    As an independent publishing house, creative studio and artist collective we build the logistical networks necessary to circulate knowledge — and then use these very systems as the subject of our own art and research practice.

    Our practice also extends to printing, training programmes, workshops, and supporting ideas through different stages of development, through knowledge exchange and advisory that help turn them into actionable projects.

    The work is collaborative — open to interventions, invitations, and the imagining of other trajectories and other futures.

    Projects

    Each project at merath begins as a question — tied to a memory, a place, a human relationship, or a cultural phenomenon that deserves documentation and renewed interpretation. Thirteen inquiries currently shape the practice, each unfolding through research, collaboration, and form.

    Publications

    Publishing is a core practice that shapes merath. In the independent press, we choose what we print, how we print it, and what kind of object the printed thing wants to be — books, manuals and editions moving across eight series.

    Programmes

    Workshops, talks and public events are the mechanisms through which we engage our community — learning opportunities and a space to think and experiment collectively, in the studio in Tripoli and well beyond it.

    Art & Production

    Our mission began by wanting to make work: we use the resources we build as a collective to make artworks, films, performance pieces and other creative productions, and to carry them to audiences across the region.

    Studies

    We publish reports and visions for the future of cultural infrastructure and museum development — research commissioned and self-initiated, offered as working tools for institutions and practitioners alike.

    Archives

    Archives are central to what we do — organising, cataloguing and preserving private and larger collections so they can be shared as public resources, and building the tools that keep them alive.

    For new projects, suggestions for archival analysis or general inquiries, connect with us: info [at] merath.ly
